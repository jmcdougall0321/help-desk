NOV/Agency Visit Overview

Within the NOV/Agency Visit Module you are able to enter in Notice of Violation incident records, add and maintain ‘NOV Types’ and ‘Regulatory Agencies’ to categorize records. Additional modules items include:

  • NOV Types – Adding and maintaining any values to classify the NOV Type
  • Regulatory Agency – Adding and maintaining any values to identify the Regulatory Agency involved.

 

NOV/Agency Visit Module – NOV/Agency Visits Module Item

NOV/Agency Visits Module Item:

Within the NOV/Agency Visits Module it is mirrored by all other Incident Types the dynamic search and display showing all previously entered incidents.

What is unique about our search grid is by selecting the heading at the top of the each column you can sort the records alphabetically or by date in the column selected. You can search for a specific record or group of records by on Location, Incident Date, etc.

 

The system supports a ‘wild card’ search, simply by putting a % symbol in front of and behind the partial work you have entered (example: %Diego%).

 

 

Adding a New Incident Record:

  •  Select the ‘Add’ button
  • Once the Add button has been selected the Incident Location Form will populate.
  • The Organizational Structure will populate to allow filtering when selecting a Location, in the ‘Incident Location’ Dropdown
  • Selecting a Employee can be done through the Search by field. Finding an Employee can be done by Last Name or Employee Number.
  • Once these fields have been provided click the ‘OK’ grid button on the bottom of the form.

 

The Incident Form displays the following fields throughout the entering of the record:

  • Employee Name
  • Case Number (Auto generated)
  • Created By
  • Created On

    *Please note these fields are all reportable fields.

    General Details Main Tab:

    This tab is used to capture the general information regarding the Incident on a broad spectrum.

    General Details Main Tab - Location Details:

     

    • Work Location - Defaulted to Location that was selected in previous Location. This location can modified by using the ‘Edit’ button to change the Incident Location.
    • Claim Number - Field available for your Worker’s Comp TPA. This can be manually entered or populated by an automated feed.
    • Case Status - Dropdown field available to determine the status of Case (example: Open, Closed, Under Review)
    • Major Event - Optional Data field that is used for capturing general incident information and allocating it across multiple modules to associate common dates, times, location and general details of a major event.
    • Location Code - Additional unique identifier used when submitting claims.
    • Claim Type - Identifying if this claim will be a 1st Party Vehicle or 3rd Party Vehicle
    • Incident Level - Pick list that is client defined, as are all pick list values throughout the application. The “what’s this” button can be selected to display your incident severity levels and their corresponding descriptions
    • Accident Occur on Employer Premises? - Yes/No Radio Button

General Details Main Tab - Dates, Times and Preparer Information:

      • Date of Incident and Date Reported to Employer – Required Fields; Select date by Calendar Control function available.
      • Incident Time and Time Reported to Employer – Select Incident Time by Time Drop downs available for accuracy.
      • Work Shift – Picklist that is client defined to identify which shift the incident took place on.
      • Part of Shift – Dropdown available to identify the part of shift that the incident took place on.
      • Preparer First Name and Preparer Last Name - Auto populated by the system based on the username and login of the person creating this incident record.
      • Preparer Phone and Preparer Title - Auto populated by the system based on the username and login of the person creating this incident record.

 

 

General Details Main Tab - General Details:

 

  • Work Station - Picklist that is client defined to identify the are that incident occurred

  • Brief Description – Text box area available for a 250 character limit of additional information related to the incident.

Causal Factors Main Tab:

The Causal Factors Main tab is the first investigative tab which allows you to use a series of text boxes and pick lists to assist in the investigation of the Immediate and underlying causes of this incident.

 

Causal Factors Main Tab - Immediate and Underlying Causes

    • Immediate Cause of the accident? - Open text box available for details on the incident investigation
    • Unsafe Condition – Picklist dropdown, the available values that are populated are added into the Unsafe Conditions Module Item within the Administration Module.
    • Unsafe Act – Picklist dropdown, the available values that are populated are added into the Unsafe Acts Module Item within the Administration Module.
    • Underlying Cause of the Accident - Open text box available for details on the incident investigation
    • Job Factors – Picklist dropdown, the available values that are populated are added into the Job Factors Module Item within the Administration Module.
    • Personal Factors – Picklist dropdown, the available values that are populated are added into the Personal Factors Module Item within the Administration Module.

Root Cause Main Tab:

The Root Cause main tab is the first of 2 investigative tabs for this incident. The Root Cause Main tab allows you to declare your problem statement by establishing your 5 Why tree. We have actually taken this out to 10 Why for your investigation. These are all free form text boxes.

 

Root Cause Main Tab - Immediate and Underlying Causes

    • Problem Statement - There are 10 Statement open text boxes available on this form
    • Why Statement – There are 10 Why Statement open text boxes available on this form

 

 

 

 

 

Citation/Violation Details Main Tab:

  • This tab will display all added Citations or Violations for this Incident. To add any additional violations or citations there is the ‘Add Citation/Violation’ form action that will have a new ‘Citation/Violation Details’ form pop up.
  • Issuing Agency – MSHA – Add or Edit and Citation/Violation fees or costs. If there are existing details these will populate in this section.

 

5 Why Main Tab:

 

The 5 Why Main Tab is a new tab that allows you to declare your problem statement, while using a pick list to categorize each of you why statements. The 5 Why pick list is populated from the 5 Why Category Module Item within the Administration Module.

  • There is no limit to the amount of “Why” paths that you can add for this incident case. Simply click the Add Why Path to create a new path.

 

 

What Happened Mini Tab

  • This is a text field used to give a description of the incident in greater detail due to the larger amount of text than allowed in “Give a Brief Description” text box.

     

    Cost Hours Mini Tab

  • This tab provides the user the ability to view any cost information associated with this incident. The cost information can be pulled back from the TPA and populated here automatically, or can be entered manually based on the users profile level. Additionally, the reserve for this incident can be assigned and tracked within this mini tab.

     

     

     

    Files/Updates Mini Tab

  • This is where supporting documentation regarding this incident can be attached. Attachments can include pictures, videos, scanned documents, pdf’s and more. By selecting the “View Valid File Types” button, you can see all file formats that are supported in this tab.
  • Any updates that the user would like to manually enter in, there is a free form text box available.

    Files Mini Tab

  •  Available for upload of any supporting documentation that is related to the Incident Record. The available valid file types can be viewed in the below button ‘View Valid File Types’

     

    Updates Mini Tab

  • Any updates that the user would like to manually enter in, there is a free form text box available.

 

 

 

Communications Mini Tab

  • In addition to automated email alerts you can broadcast an incident report to a group of people via an email, directly from this tab. Select the person or group of people you would like to receive the email and attach files from the “Files” mini tab using point and click check boxes.

Corrective Actions Mini Tab

  • There are several modules in the system available to assign a corrective action that is enforced through a series of automated emails and alerts. The controls that governs the coming due, escalation and past due emails and alerts are client defined within the Alerts Module and Alerts Module Item. Once you have completed your initial set up, they will fire off automatically as applicable. All corrective actions can be updated directly from an email, you do not have to log into the system to update the statuses.

     

     

     

     

    Review/Approval Mini Tab

    • This is where you can set up a review/ approval process, using a series of email alerts and reminders, for an incident to go through the different levels of review necessary with an automated process. The review process is set up in the Administration Module within the Review Process Module Item.

      History Mini Tab

    • This feature will show you any/all other incidents the employee has been involved in with your organization since the start of their employment. You can view the historical incident details by selecting the incident record in the display grid and select the “View” button.

      People Involved Mini Tab

      •  This is where information from a witnesses, first responders, or any other person/people that may be associated with the incident can be captured. Record who they are, how to get a hold of them, and their statement.
