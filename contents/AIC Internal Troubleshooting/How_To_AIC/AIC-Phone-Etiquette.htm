Phone Etiquette

You represent AIC every time you answer the phone

  • Even when you know who is on the other end (Caller ID)
    • The person on the other end of the line could be a director or vice president and we need to speak to them according
      • Respect their position (Will serve you well)
      • Don’t ever assume anything (Will get you in trouble)
  • Consistency is key (Not Robotic)
  • Always answer the phone with a professional manner
    • Cannot error on professionalism

Your goal is to control every call that comes through

  • Maintain the direction of the call
  • Controlling the call helps reduce your anxiety and instills trust in our clients

Each call only has two directions:

  1. Service in Support: Problem or a Fix
    1. If something needs to be fixed you need to get the necessary information from the client so we know exactly what needs to be fixed
        1. Create Bug/Task in TFS
  2. Information: Help in the Application
    1. You need to figure out what they are doing, what Browser they are in, etc. all information Dev. needs to know/information that will help you troubleshoot
    2. You will go in to investigation mode to help problem solve
    3. Have them walk you through the steps that they are taking so you know exactly what they are doing and if it’s an issue
      1. Training: If somebody needs to be taught something then you need to find out what it is that they don't understand and you need to work with them to schedule a time so that you can train them in the application
        1. All about listening to what they want
        2. The system isn’t broken – the client isn’t using the application correctly
        3. If they need training:
          1. Help the Client over the phone
            1. Make sure you know what Profile Level they have (Admin)
              1. If not Admin – have them contact their Site Admin – they can contact you (AIC) for additional End User Training
          2. Provide them dates/times to set up a WebEx for formal Training

Every call should end with the customer knowing exactly what's going to happen next and a ticket should be created

  • Gemini
  • TFS

After every phone call follow up with an email.

  1. To recap the conversation, so nothing goes un-noticed.
  2. If there needs to be a follow up, this is a good reminder to yourself as well as the client.
