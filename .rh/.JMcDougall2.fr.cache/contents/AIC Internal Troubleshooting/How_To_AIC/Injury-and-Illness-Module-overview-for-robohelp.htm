Injury and Illness Module Injury and Illness Module Overviewand Illness Module Overview The Injury/Illness Module is one of the most commonly used Modules. It serves as the basis of our Incident Management suite. Within this Module you are able to report on the incident records you have created, populate OSHA Logs, and submit claims to your TPA’s.   In this Module you will enter of the information that you have gathered regarding an injury/illness incident. As you will see all of the modules and module items have a common user interface. When adding or editing a record there will be a series of tabs across the top of the incident form that will contain required data fields to save or maintain the record and a series of tabs across the bottom of the incident form for collecting additional information or attachments.   Injury and Illness Module Item: Within the Injury/Illness Module Item you will see a dynamic search and display grid showing all previously entered incidents in the Injury and Illness module. What is unique about our search grid is by selecting the heading at the top of each column you can sort the records alphabetically or by date in the column selected. You can also search for a specific record using multiple columns to narrow down your search. Our system also supports wild card searches by putting a % symbol in front of and behind the partial word you have entered (ex: %Diego%).   Adding an Incident Record: When adding a new record follow the steps below: Select the Add button, the Incident Type and Location Form will populate All required fields (marked with a red asterisk) must be complete before you can move onto the next step. Select the Type of report which is automatically populated in the dropdown. Next you can narrow down your location search by opening up your organizational structure. Once you find the group or level you are looking for click on it. All the locations available will automatically populate in the Select Incident Location drop down. Select the location for this record. The Final step is to select an employee for whom the record is being created for. The employees that automatically populate here are those who are assigned this location as their work location in the People Module item within the Administration Module. You will search for the employee by last name or employee number. In the search criteria field insert a letter or part of a name, all the employees that match the search criteria for that location will automatically populate in the employee dropdown. If you cannot find the employee you are looking for you are able to create a people record on the fly by clicking the Add New Employee Button. The employee details input form will populate for you to fill in the required information. Click save and close to return to the Incident Type and Location Form. Once the employee is selected or created click ok to move onto the Incident Form. General Details Main Tab: This tab is used to capture the general information regarding this incident on a broad spectrum. All of the required data fields have a red asterisk next to their title. The work location is auto populated based upon the work location that was selected on the Incident Type and Location form. If the employee related to this record is a traveling employee, you can select the edit button to change the incident location. There is a field for capturing a claim number from your Worker’s Comp TPA. This can be manually entered or populated by an automated data feed from your TPA. The case status is a pick list used for describing this particular case’s status. The Major Event pick list is an optional data field. To populate this drop down a major event incident must be added to the Major Event Incidents Module Item within the Administration Module. This dropdown is used for capturing general incident information and allocating it across multiple modules to associate common dates, times, location and general details of a major event. Example: There is a fire on the manufacturing floor, which causes a spill, which results in a slip/ trip/ fall injury of and employee. These are 3 separate incidents, but 1 major event. There are additional pick lists for Location Code and Claim Type. Next we have the Incident Level pick list that is client defined, as are all pick list values throughout the application. The “what’s this” button can be selected to display your incident severity levels and their corresponding descriptions. To change the description of this pick list or the levels contact AIC Support. Severity Consequence Matrix Date of Incident, Incident Time, Date Reported to Employer, and Time Reported to Employer can be selected by clicking on the time or date field, you will see a calendar control or time drop down for accuracy purposes. Just click the date you would like to choose and it will auto populate the field. There is additional fields to tie this incident to a Work Shift, or a Part of Shift. The work shift field is populated in the Work Shift Module Item within the Administration Module. All of the required Preparer fields are auto populated by the system based on the username and login of the person creating this incident record. That person’s credentials will remain with the incident even if it is edited later by someone else. The final pick list on this tab is the Work Station/ Area validation table. This list is also made up of client defined values. To the right is a text box for associating the work station/ area to a specific location the incident took place. The final required field is the Give a Brief Description of the Incident text box that allows for additional information related to the incident. (100 Character Max.) There is a separate text box for documenting the Employee/ Contractor statement. (1000 Character Max.)   The information reported in this tab will flow to the OSHA 301, 300 Log and 300A Summary and BLS 9300 in their exact formats when generating reports. Employee Details Main Tab: The employee Details main tab integrates with the people records created in the Administration Module to populate all of the Employee details. The display of this information is profile driven, which allows these fields to be hidden from view based upon the end users security level. If the Employee Details are not for the correct employee you can click the Search for New Employee button to search for a different employee. The Employee Details are automatically populated for First Name, Last name, Middle initial, Employee number, SSN, date of hire, Job title, Department, and supervisor based on the person selected on the Incident Type and Location Form. There are two required fields on this tab. The first is the Drug Test Given. The radio button defaults to NO, so the requirements are fulfilled automatically. If the Yes radio button is selected for Drug Test you will be required to select the appropriate test given from the Test Given pick list. This pick list is populated from the Drug Test Module Item within the Incident Reporting Module. The date and time of the test will also need to be filled in. The second required field is the Alcohol Test Given. The radio button is also defaulted to NO, so the requirements are fulfilled automatically. If Yes is selected for Alcohol Test you will be required to record the date and time of the test. Work Details that are captured in this tab include the time that the employee began work, the date that they started their current position, the task that was being performed, the EE’s experience with that task, how often the EE performs the task, the part/model number that they were working with at the time of the incident, and any relevant EE Hobbies. None of these items are required however they provide extra information for those who want it. The information reported in this tab will flow to the OSHA 301, 300 Log and 300A Summary and BLS 9300 in their exact formats when generating reports. What- How Main Tab: The What- How main tab collects the information on what happened and treatment information for the employee. The basic Treatment Information is collected using a series of radio buttons. The only one required is: Was medical treatment provided on site. If No is selected the fulfillments are met. If Yes is selected 2 additional required text boxes appear, the treatment provided and the provided by text boxes. The Hospital/Treatment Facility pick list is for choosing where the employee went for treatment. This drop down is populated in the Hospitals Module Item within the Incident Reporting Module. The Physician/Health Care Professional pick list is for choosing the person who attended to the employee. This drop down is populated in the Physicians Module Item within the Incident Reporting Module. The system is set up to only populate the Hospital/Treatment Facilities and Physician/Health Care Professionals that are associated with the location associated with that record. The 4 text boxes under the Describe in Detail What Happened section allow you to record information that will flow directly to the OSHA 301 and First Report of injury. The Object/Substance pick list refers to what was involved in injuring the employee. This pick list is populated by the Object/Substance Module Item within the Administration Module. The Accident Type pick list refers to the type of accident the employee endured. This pick list is populated by the Injury Event Module Item within the Administration Module. The Agency/ Source of Injury pick list refers to reason for the incident. This pick list is populated by the Injury Source Module Item within the Administration Module. The Specify the primary body part for reporting purposes is made up of 3 pick lists which are used to record what body part on the employee where the incident took place. The Body Part pick lists is populated in the Body Part Module Item within the Administration Module. The Nature of Injury pick list is populated within the Nature of Injury Module Item within the Administration Module. The Add button is used if an employee was injured on more than one body part. When added the additional body parts that were injured would be included to this incident record for precision in your reporting.   The information reported in this tab will flow to the OSHA 301, 300 Log and 300A Summary and BLS 9300 in their exact formats when generating reports. Recordability Main Tab: The recordability main tab allows you the flexibility to easily record both recordable and non-recordable incident information by selecting the appropriate radio button. In the Non Recordable section, if Frist Aid is selected, a pick list will populate allowing you to select the appropriate form of first aid that was administered. The 14 forms of first aid are provided by OSHA. In the Recordable Incidents section, you can select if this incident is an: Injury Illness Privacy case Sharp/ Needle stick Fatality Override OSHA Recordability Loss of Consciousness If any other treatment other than first aid (that is recordable) If you are aware of any issues that make you question if this injury is work related and If there was a pre- existing condition.   The logic set up in our system is based upon whether you selected this case to be recordable or non- recordable when creating this incident record. If the record was chosen to be set up as an injury in the Incident Type and Location Form and then was changed to a non-recordable in the recordability main tab, the system will stop you and prompt you with a message that states: “By changing this case to Non Recordable, any Days Away or Restricted Duty days away will be lost. Do you wish to continue?”   The information reported in this tab will flow to the OSHA 301, 300 Log and 300A Summary and BLS 9300 in their exact formats when generating reports. Root Cause Main Tab: The Root Cause main tab is the first of 2 investigative tabs for this incident. The Root Cause Main tab allows you to declare your problem statement by establishing your 5 Why tree. We have actually taken this out to 10 Why for your investigation. These are all free form text boxes. 5 Why Main Tab: The 5 Why Main Tab is a new tab that allows you to declare your problem statement, while using a pick list to categorize each of you why statements. The 5 Why pick list is populated from the 5 Why Category Module Item within the Administration Module. There is no limit to the amount of “Why” paths that you can add for this incident case. Simply click the Add Why Path to create a new path. Causal Factors Main Tab: The Causal Factors Main tab is the second investigative tab which allows you to use a series of text boxes and pick lists to assist in the investigation of the Immediate and underlying causes of this incident.   Here we can identify the following: The immediate cause of the accident with a free form text box. The Unsafe Conditions pick list is populated by the Unsafe Conditions Module Item within the Administration Module. The Unsafe Acts pick list is populated by the Unsafe Acts Module Item within the Administration Module. The underlying cause of the accident with a free form text box. The Job Factors pick list is populated by the Job Factors Module Item within the Administration Module. The Personal Factors pick list is populated by the Personal Factors Module Item within the Administration Module. The failure in the safety management system pick list is populated by the Failure in System Module Item within the Administration Module If the job is covered by a JSA/ work instruction with a radio button. ‎‎Wage Insurance Disability Main Tab The wage Insurance Disability Main tab is the first of 2 tabs that display the Workers Compensation capabilities of the system. The employee wage information can be pulled from the client HRIS system and populated into the Employee Wage Information section.   In the employee disability information section the point and click calendar controls make it simple to record the Day last Worked and the Day that Disability Began. There are radio buttons to acknowledge if the employee was paid in full for the day of the injury.   The final section of this tab contains the Insurance and Coverage Information. Here we can identify, using localized pick lists: The Carrier pick list is populated by the Carriers Module Item within the Incident Reporting Module. The Agent pick list is populated by the Agent Module Item within the Incident Reporting Module. The Administrator pick list is populated by the Claims Administrators Module Item within the Incident Reporting Module. The Adjuster pick list is populated by the Claims Adjuster Module Item within the Incident Reporting Module. The (State) FROI Main Tab: The First Report of Injury (FROI) main tab is dynamically created by our application and appears for the state based upon the location that was selected when creating the incident record. Each State has different requirements creating each FROI to be slightly different. What Happens, Review Approval Process, and Cost/Hours Mini Tab   What Happened Mini Tab: This is a text field used to give a description of the incident in greater detail due to the larger amount of text than allowed in “Give a Brief Description” text box. Review Approval Process Mini Tab: This is where you can set up a review/ approval process, using a series of email alerts and reminders, for an incident to go through the different levels of review necessary with an automated process. The review process is set up in the Administration Module within the Review Process Module Item. Cost/Hours Mini Tab: This tab provides the user the ability to view any cost information associated with this incident. The cost information can be pulled back from the TPA and populated here automatically, or can be entered manually based on the users profile level. Additionally, the reserve for this incident can be assigned and tracked within this mini tab. Files, People Involved, and Corrective Actions Mini Tab Files Mini Tab: This is where supporting documentation regarding this incident can be attached. Attachments can include pictures, videos, scanned documents, pdf’s and more. By selecting the “View Valid File Types” button, you can see all file formats that are supported in this tab. People Involved Mini Tab: This is where information from a witnesses, first responders, or any other person/people that may be associated with the incident can be captured. Record who they are, how to get a hold of them, and their statement. Corrective Actions Mini Tab: Anywhere in the system you can assign a corrective action that are enforced through a series of automated emails and alerts. The controls that govern the coming due, escalation and past due emails and alerts are client defined within the Alerts Module and Alerts Module Item. Once you have completed your initial set up, they will fire off automatically as applicable. All corrective actions can be updated directly from an email, you do not have to log into the system to update the statuses. ‎‎Communications Mini Tab In addition to automated email alerts you can broadcast an incident report to a group of people via an email, directly from this tab. Select the person or group of people you would like to receive the email and attach files from the “Files” mini tab using point and click check boxes. To set up the names or groups who can receive these emails are done in the Administration Module within the Communications Module Item. The communications is set up based on location. Once a location is selected the list of recipients can be created by individuals which can then create groups. To upload individuals there is an Upload Wizard button which gives you step by step directions to load more than one person at a time. Updates, Days Away/Restricted, and History Mini Tab Updates Mini Tab: Is an electronic diary that records any date/time stamps and any updates recorded in this tab. Days Away/Restricted Mini Tab: This tab is calendar controlled and can start and stop counting the days away/restricted as many times as you need. It will count up to/capping at 180 days per OSHA, giving proper weight to days away vs. restricted. For a WC case it will keep counting days until the case is closed. History Mini Tab: This feature will show you any/all other incidents the employee has been involved in with your organization since the start of their employment. You can view the historical incident details by selecting the incident record in the display grid and select the “View” button. General Details Main Tab: This tab is used to capture the general information regarding this incident on a broad spectrum. All of the required data fields have a red asterisk next to their title.     The work location is auto populated based upon the work location that was selected on the Incident Type and Location form. If the employee related to this record is a traveling employee, you can select the edit button to change the incident location. There is a field for capturing a claim number from your Worker’s Comp TPA. This can be manually entered or populated by an automated data feed from your TPA. The case status is a pick list used for describing this particular case’s status. The Major Event pick list is an optional data field. To populate this drop down a major event incident must be added to the Major Event Incidents Module Item within the Administration Module. This dropdown is used for capturing general incident information and allocating it across multiple modules to associate common dates, times, location and general details of a major event. Example: There is a fire on the manufacturing floor, which causes a spill, which results in a slip/ trip/ fall injury of and employee. These are 3 separate incidents, but 1 major event. There are additional pick lists for Location Code and Claim Type. Next we have the Incident Level pick list that is client defined, as are all pick list values throughout the application. The “what’s this” button can be selected to display your incident severity levels and their corresponding descriptions. To change the description of this pick list or the levels contact AIC Support. Date of Incident, Incident Time, Date Reported to Employer, and Time Reported to Employer can be selected by clicking on the time or date field, you will see a calendar control or time drop down for accuracy purposes. Just click the date you would like to choose and it will auto populate the field. There is additional fields to tie this incident to a Work Shift, or a Part of Shift. The work shift field is populated in the Work Shift Module Item within the Administration Module. All of the required Preparer fields are auto populated by the system based on the username and login of the person creating this incident record. That person’s credentials will remain with the incident even if it is edited later by someone else. The final pick list on this tab is the Work Station/ Area validation table. This list is also made up of client defined values. To the right is a text box for associating the work station/ area to a specific location the incident took place. The final required field is the Give a Brief Description of the Incident text box that allows for additional information related to the incident. (100 Character Max.) There is a separate text box for documenting the Employee/ Contractor statement. (1000 Character Max.) Employee Details Main Tab: The Employee Details main tab integrates with the people records created in the Administration Module to populate all of the Employee details. The display of this information is profile driven, which allows these fields to be hidden from view based upon the end users security level. If the Employee Details are not for the correct employee you can click the Search for New Employee button to search for a different employee. The Employee Details are automatically populated for First Name, Last name, Middle initial, Employee number, SSN, date of hire, Job title, Department, and supervisor based on the person selected on the Incident Type and Location Form. Work Details that are captured in this tab include the time that the employee began work, the date that they started their current position, the task that was being performed, the EE’s experience with that task, how often the EE performs the task, the part/model number that they were working with at the time of the incident, and any relevant EE Hobbies.
